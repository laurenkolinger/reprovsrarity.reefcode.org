Manuscript values

Every derived number, read live from the saved analysis objects

Last updated

July 5, 2026

Olinger LK, Edmunds PJ, Levitan D, Smith TB, Lasker H, Feeley M, Mahoney L, Dahl A. Reproductive mode, but not rarity, influences population trajectories in corals. In Review 2026.

This page collects every derived value the manuscript reports and computes each one live from the saved analysis objects, so no number on this page is typed by hand. Each analysis page writes its fitted model, its coefficient table, its group slopes, and its per-taxon descriptive table to a timestamped RData file (Step 14 of each analysis). This page loads the most recent file for each program and reads the values straight out of those objects.

The values appear twice: once for the as-published range, which reproduces the numbers in the manuscript, and once for the updated range, which reruns the identical analysis on the full monitoring record and therefore differs where new survey years have been added. Each section is labeled with the range it came from.

As published (through 2023)

The common-broadcaster cover half-life is 16 years at TCRMP, 30 at VINPS, and 26 at CSUN (the years for the group to lose half its cover at the fitted rate). The three-way Year x Rarity x Reproductive-mode interaction is significant at VINPS (p = 0.008), marginal at CSUN (p = 0.049), and not significant at TCRMP (p = 0.150). Within brooders alone, the Year x Rarity interaction is significant at VINPS (p = 0.002) and CSUN (p = 0.001) but not at TCRMP (p = 0.573); within broadcasters the two rarity classes decline in parallel. Across TCRMP and VINPS, 14 of 41 baseline species were lost by the end of the record (34%), and CSUN lost 4 of 17 genera (24%); every lost taxon was classified rare (all rare). The octocoral study window runs 2014 to 2024, a span of 10 years.

Updated since publication (through the latest survey year)

On the full monitoring record, the common-broadcaster cover half-life is 15 years at TCRMP, 26 at VINPS, and 26 at CSUN. The three-way interaction reads p = 0.003 at VINPS, p = 0.049 at CSUN, and p = 0.078 at TCRMP. Within brooders, the Year x Rarity interaction reads p < 0.001 at VINPS, p = 0.001 at CSUN, and p = 0.378 at TCRMP. Across TCRMP and VINPS, 16 of 41 baseline species were lost by the most recent period (39%), and CSUN lost 4 of 17 genera (24%). The octocoral study window now runs 2014 to 2024, a span of 10 years. These numbers differ from the published section because the range extends past 2023.
